Portfolio Risk Management

A core concept of the portfolio management model is the balance between risk and return. According to the Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH), in order to generate more returns, more risk must be taken. Therefore, our goal is to find a group of portfolios that have the highest return given the risk. These portfolios are on the so-called Efficient Frontier and Efficient Portfolio. Thus, to restate our goal, we want to use a model to find the efficient frontier.

I will compose a simple portfolio for demonstration. This portfolio will contain seven stocks, including:

  1. Ford Motor
  2. Tesla
  3. Nike
  4. Coca-Cola Company
  5. Amazon
  6. Adobe
  7. Google

In reality, a portfolio is generally more diverse than this one (in order to get exposed to more industries and lower the risk). But for the simplicity and educational goal, these will be all of the assets we have in the portfolio.
