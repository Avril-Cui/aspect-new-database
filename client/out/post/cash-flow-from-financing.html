Cash Flow From Financing

What is Cash Flow From Financing?

Cash flow from financing implies the amount of cash the business raises from or spends on finance-related activities.

A typical example of cash flow from financing is dividends payment. Many companies are paying out dividends to their investors as a reward for their shareholders. Dividends can act as a useful strategy for a company to reward and attract investors.

Another example of cash flow from financing is bond issuance. Many companies are issuing long-term bonds for investors to purchase. Both equities (i.e. stocks) and debt (i.e. bonds) are sources for a company to raise capital and increase their cash on hand.
