PESTEL

What is PESTEL?

PESTEL is a qualitative, fundamental evaluating framework to measure the external environments (or the so-called macro analysis). PESTEL is frequently used by both business managers and investors. For managers, they use PESTEL to break down their business strategy to see if their business will succeed given the external situations. For investors, PESTEL is important as it helps to measure the macroeconomic advantages and disadvantages of a company and assists in selecting the industry or company to invest in. PESTEL also provides a snapshot of the companyâ€™s resilience under exceptional macroeconomic conditions. PESTEL helps seek the risks and opportunities from a broad perspective. It also embedded the ESG (environmental, social, governance) (Link to Why Is ESG Important?) factors, a popular approach in recent years. Some key points derived by PESTEL can work together with the two other frameworks, known as SWOT and Porter's Five Forces.
