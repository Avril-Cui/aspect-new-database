Elon Musk Confirmed Tesla’s 3.5% Job Cut

What Happened To Tesla?

Tesla, the car manufacturer giant, has over 110,000 employees around the globe. It is known that in early June, the Tesla CEO Elon Musk wrote an internal email to the employees, expressing his “super bad feeling” about the future economy. Later in the month, Musk confirmed that he would start cutting Tesla’s salaried workers, accounting for 3.5% of Tesla’s total workforce. He also added that Tesla will gradually increase the proportion of hourly staffing.

Photo source: https://www.teslarati.com/tesla-employee-headcount-2021/.

What Are The Reasons Behind?

The cut has influenced many departments under Tesla, including human resources representatives and software engineers. One major reason behind this huge job cut is to increase Tesla’s profitability and business efficiency. In his letter, Musk emphasized, “the office must be where your actual colleagues are located, not some remote pseudo office. If you don’t show up, we will assume you have resigned.” He also commended Tesla’s Shanghai Gigafactory’s efficiency. Many employees had lived and worked in the factory during Shanghai’s lockdown to keep the production line going. Therefore, one crucial explanation behind Tesla’s decision is to cut unnecessary expenses and encourage (or urge) its employees to work harder and more efficiently.

Photo source: https://www.technologistan.pk/elon-musk-asks-tesla-employees-to-return-to-office-or-leave/.

Deeper Breakdown: Why Hourly Employee?

Another critical point to notice is that at the same time, Tesla has decided to increase its number of hourly employees. Hence the difference between salaried and hourly workers could potentially be an essential motivation behind Tesla’s decision.

Salaried Employee

A salaried employee receives a fixed amount of salary per month (or per year). Most salaried employees are paid for 40 hours of work per week, but hours over or less this amount will not cause changes in the fixed salary. A benefit of salaried employees is that since the salary is pre-determined, the company does not need to pay more to workers who work more than 40 hours. However, if workers are frequently late to jobs or do not work enough hours per week, hiring salaried employees can be a loss for the company.

Hourly Employee

An hourly employee receives a salary based on a specific rate per hour of working. Hourly employees will also receive an additional paid rate for working time exceeding 40 hours. Although companies are required to pay more to their employees (if employees work more than 40 hours), hiring hourly employees can be benefitial since companies can eliminate unnecessary expenses paid to unqualified workers (workers not reaching the required work time).

Analysis On The Job Cut

After understanding the advantages and deficiencies behind salaried employees and hourly employees, we can find some clues behind Tesla’s job cut. In Musk’s letter, he keeps emphasizing “working efficiency” and his disappointments for employees not coming to the job or being late. Therefore, cutting salaried workers and switching to hourly employees can be a solution for Tesla to eliminate the above situations. Besides, since hourly employees will receive more payment if they work more than 40 hours, accepting more hourly employees could potentially be a way that Tesla used to encourage its workers to work longer and harder.
